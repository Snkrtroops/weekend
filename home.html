Nike Blazers

Nike debuted the Blazer in 1973 as a performance basketball sneaker, mounting a leather upper and mesh nylon tongue atop a textured vulcanized rubber sole.

Nike Air Force 1

The Nike Air Force 1 remains a popular retro shoe to this day. The Air Force 1 features an upper that is most frequently dressed in leather, a large Swoosh across the lateral and medial sides, perforated toe boxes, metal lace dubraes, and an Air-encapsulated midsole with signature “AIR” text near the rear.

Nike Uptempo

The Nike Air More Uptempo '96 is inspired by the high-flying basketball style of the '80s and '90s. With its distinctive branding and visible cushioning, there's no mistaking its honourable place in sneakerhead history. Real and synthetic leather adds durability. Multiple Air-Sole units give you lightweight cushioning.

Nike Cortez

CORTEZ KENNY IV For this new design, Kendrick Lamar drew inspiration from the traditional house slipper worn by many LA natives. The plush quilted insole provides a fit unlike any other Cortez, and bold HOUSE SHOES detail on the tongue continues Lamar's modern design language on the timeless silhouette.
